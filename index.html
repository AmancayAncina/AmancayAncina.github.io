01 / Research

Understanding how people navigate information online.

I am a media and social psychologist and doctoral student at the University of Duisburg-Essen. My work examines why people believe, assess, and share misinformation, and how psychological interventions can strengthen informed decision-making in social media environments.

My work has so far focused primarily on messenger communication, Instagram, and TikTok. I investigate how assessments of misinformation are shaped by motivated reasoning and critical thinking abilities, and how inoculation and prebunking strategies can help mitigate its spread.

Within the BMFTR-funded project VAMPIR, I investigate video-based misinformation and deepfakes, with a focus on strengthening the resilience of adolescents and young adults.
